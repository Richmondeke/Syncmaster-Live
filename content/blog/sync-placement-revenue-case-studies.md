## What Made It Possible

Landing a sync placement isn't about luck; it's about preparedness and access. For our composers, several key factors converged:

1.  **Professionally Produced Music:** The foundation of any sync placement is high-quality audio. Our composers consistently deliver production-ready tracks.
2.  **Rights Clarity:** This is non-negotiable. Global supervisors need to know they can clear rights quickly and cleanly. SyncMaster vets composers to ensure they own 100% of their masters and publishing, and that there are no uncleared samples. This "one-stop" clearance is a massive advantage.
3.  **Metadata Perfection:** Every track must be meticulously tagged. This includes ISRC codes, BPM, key, genre, mood, and instrumentation. Without this, supervisors can't find your music, and if they do, they can't easily license it. SyncMaster ensures this is handled correctly. 4.  **Stems Prepared:** For many sync uses, especially in film and TV, supervisors need individual track stems (e.g., drums, bass, vocals, synths). SyncMaster ensures composers have these ready. ## What It Paid

The financial impact of sync licensing is often vastly underestimated by independent artists. While streaming royalties can be minuscule, sync fees offer substantial income.

*   **Premium TV Ad Placement:** A single 30-second placement in a premium TV advertisement can generate anywhere from **$10,000 to $75,000**. This fee is for a limited license, often for just a few months or a year.
*   **Netflix Scene Placement:** For a placement in a single scene on a major streaming platform like Netflix, composers can expect fees ranging from **$5,000 to $20,000**. This is for a track that might be heard for just a minute or two.
*   **Global NGO Campaign:** The placement for the Lagos-based composer in the global NGO campaign generated a fee significantly higher than what they would earn from thousands of streams over several months. This type of placement often involves broader usage rights, reflecting the campaign's reach.

These figures highlight a critical truth: **sync licensing is where the real money is for composers**. The global sync market is a multi-billion dollar industry, and African music is increasingly a sought-after asset within it.

## How to Put Yourself in the Same Position

Getting your music placed globally requires a strategic approach. It's about making your catalog as accessible and "cleared for use" as possible.

1.  **Audit Your Catalog:** Go through your existing music. Do you own 100% of the masters and publishing? Are there any uncleared samples? If there are rights issues, can they be resolved? If not, focus on creating new material that is rights-clean from the start.
2.  **Master Your Metadata:** Ensure every track has a unique ISRC code, accurate BPM, key, genre, and descriptive tags for mood, instrumentation, and context. This is the backbone of discoverability.
3.  **Organize Your Stems:** Create folders for each track containing separate audio files for drums, bass, guitars, vocals, synths, etc. Export them at a high sample rate (e.g., 48kHz/24bit WAV).
4.  **Build Your Rights Documentation:** Have clear agreements with any collaborators and be ready to provide proof of ownership.
