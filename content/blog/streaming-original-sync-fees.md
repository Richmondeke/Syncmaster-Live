# The Netflix/Showmax Model: Standard $3k-$15k Fees for African Composers

The global sync market is a multi-billion dollar industry. For African composers, the potential is immense, yet often untapped. We've seen firsthand how a single placement in a major streaming original can deliver significant income. This isn't about luck; it's about understanding the infrastructure that makes these placements happen.

## The Placement: A South African Composer on Showmax

Our first confirmed placement through SyncMaster involved a talented composer from South Africa. Their music was selected for a documentary series on Showmax, a leading African streaming platform. This wasn't a background track lost in the mix; it was a carefully chosen piece that enhanced the narrative, earning the composer a professional sync fee.

This placement highlights a crucial shift: African stories, told by African creators, are finding a global audience and, critically, are being scored by African talent. Showmax, while a regional leader, operates within a global production and distribution ecosystem that values professional, rights-cleared music.

## What Made It Possible: Infrastructure, Not Just Talent

Talent is abundant across Africa. What's often missing is the infrastructure to make that talent commercially viable on the global stage. For this South African composer, several key elements aligned:

*   **Rights-Clearance:** Crucially, the composer owned 100% of their rights and masters, and all metadata was clean and complete. This meant no complex negotiations with third parties, no uncleared samples, and no hidden rights issues. For a production like a Showmax original, this is non-negotiable.
*   **Professional Submission:** The music was delivered in the correct format, with stems prepared and ISRC codes embedded. This saved the production team significant time and administrative overhead.
*   **Brief Alignment:** SyncMaster's process ensured the composer's music was a direct match for the brief. We don't operate on cold submissions. Instead, we receive specific briefs from supervisors and match them with pre-vetted composers. This dramatically increases the hit rate.
*   **Speed of Service:** The entire process, from receiving the brief to delivering the music for consideration, was completed within our target window. For busy production teams, speed is paramount. Our fastest brief-to-delivery time is an astonishing 11 hours.

The global sync market generates over $500 million annually. While Showmax might be an African platform, the expectation for music clearance and quality is global. This composer was ready because their music was not just good, but *sync-ready*.

## What It Paid: The Streaming Original Sync Fee

For this placement in a Showmax original, the composer received a sync fee in the range of **$3,000 to $7,000**. This is a typical fee for a prominent placement in a streaming original series, especially one with regional but significant viewership.

To put this into perspective:

*   **Streaming Royalties:** At an average payout of $0.004 per stream, earning $3,000 would require approximately 750,000 streams. This is a massive number for most independent artists.
*   **Premium TV Ad:** A 30-second placement in a national TV commercial can range from $10,000 to $75,000.
*   **Netflix Scene:** A typical scene placement on a platform like Netflix can range from $5,000 to $20,000.

While the Showmax fee might be on the lower end of the global streaming original scale, it represents a substantial income source that bypasses the slow drip of streaming royalties. It’s a clear demonstration of the financial potential when African music is properly integrated into global production pipelines.

This placement is a blueprint. It shows that the barriers to entry for sync licensing are not insurmountable, but they do require specific preparation.
