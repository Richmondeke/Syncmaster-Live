# Solving the Infrastructure Gap: The One-Stop Model for African Media

The global sync market generates over $500 million annually. For African composers, accessing this lucrative landscape has historically been blocked by complex rights structures and a lack of direct infrastructure. SyncMaster is changing that, demonstrating how a streamlined, one-stop clearance model unlocks unprecedented opportunities for African music in global media.

## The Artist and Their Background

Consider the journey of two composers who recently secured placements through SyncMaster.

The first, a producer based in Cape Town, has been crafting intricate electronic soundscapes for years, often for local South African productions. His work is technically brilliant, deeply resonant with the continent's sonic identity, but lacked the professional polish and rights management needed for international markets. He understood the creative side but was daunted by the business complexities of sync licensing.

The second, a composer from Lagos, Nigeria, specializes in vibrant, percussive afro-fusion. He had a growing portfolio of work used in Nigerian films and commercials. While his music possessed undeniable energy and originality, he faced the common challenge of fragmented rights ownership and the absence of clean, master-ready stems, making him invisible to global music supervisors.

## The Placement or Moment

These composers, previously operating in localized markets, recently saw their music placed in significant international productions.

The Cape Town composer's track was selected for a new drama series on **Showmax**, a major African streaming platform. This placement wasn't just a credit; it was a validation of his readiness for a wider audience. The clearance process, typically a labyrinth of negotiations, was handled entirely by SyncMaster, ensuring the composer retained 100% of his rights and masters.

The Lagos-based composer secured a placement in a global NGO campaign. This project demanded music that was not only emotionally impactful but also legally sound, requiring a one-stop license. SyncMaster facilitated the entire process, delivering the cleared track within 24 hours of receiving the brief. This speed is critical in the fast-paced world of advertising and campaign production.

These aren't isolated incidents. SyncMaster has facilitated multiple placements, with a **fastest brief-to-delivery time of just 11 hours**. These successes underscore the demand for authentic African music and the viability of the one-stop model.

## What it Means for African Music Globally

Historically, clearing music from African artists for international projects has been a significant hurdle. Multiple rights holders, unclear ownership, and the absence of readily available stems and metadata created a clearance nightmare for music supervisors. This often led to them defaulting to familiar Western catalogues, overlooking the rich, diverse talent pool on the continent.

The one-stop model, as implemented by SyncMaster, directly addresses this pain point. It means a music supervisor can approach one entity – SyncMaster – and receive a fully cleared track where all necessary rights (master recording and publishing) are bundled. This drastically reduces the administrative burden and legal risk.

For African composers, this means their music is no longer an outlier; it’s a viable, accessible asset for global media. A single 30-second placement in a premium TV ad can yield between $10,000–$75,000, while a scene in a major Netflix production could earn $5,000–$20,000. These figures dwarf typical streaming royalties, offering a tangible path to sustainable income.

## The Infrastructure Lesson

The success of these composers highlights a critical infrastructure gap that SyncMaster is filling. The lesson is clear: talent and creativity are abundant across Africa, but the systems to connect that talent to global commercial opportunities have been missing.

SyncMaster’s approach is built on three pillars:

1.  **Vetting for Sync-Readiness:** Every composer on the platform is pre-vetted. This includes confirming rights ownership, ensuring metadata is clean and comprehensive (including ISRC codes), and verifying that stems are ready for immediate use. This process filters out submissions that would otherwise lead to clearance delays.
2.  **One-Stop Clearance:** By consolidating rights management, SyncMaster offers supervisors a single point of contact for licensing. This eliminates the need to track down multiple publishers and master rights holders, drastically speeding up the process. [INTERNAL LINK: What is a one-stop licence?]
3.  **Brief-Matched Submissions:** Composers don't waste time submitting music speculatively. Instead, SyncMaster matches their pre-vetted catalogue to specific music briefs received from supervisors, ensuring relevance and increasing the likelihood of a placement.

This infrastructure transforms the composer's role from a passive creator to a commercially viable partner in the global media ecosystem.
