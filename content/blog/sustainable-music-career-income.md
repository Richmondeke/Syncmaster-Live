## What It Paid

Let's talk numbers, because this is where the paradigm shift truly happens. The global sync market is a multi-billion dollar industry, with estimates placing it over $500 million annually. While streaming royalties can be painstakingly slow to accumulate, sync fees offer immediate, substantial revenue.

Consider these benchmarks:

*   **A single 30-second placement in a premium TV ad:** This can range from **$10,000 to $75,000**.
*   **A track featured in a single scene on a major streaming platform like Netflix:** This can earn anywhere from **$5,000 to $20,000**.
*   **A placement in a global brand campaign:** Fees can easily reach **$50,000 to $500,000+**.

For context, earning $5,000 from streaming royalties would require millions of plays, distributed over many months or even years. A single sync placement can achieve that value in one transaction.

## How to Put Yourself in the Same Position

The good news? You don't need a major label deal or a global agent to access these opportunities. You need the right infrastructure and the right approach.

Here’s how you can start positioning yourself for sync success:

1.  **Professionalise Your Catalogue:** Ensure every track you want to pitch for sync is:
    *   **Rights-Clear:** No uncleared samples, no third-party master recording claims. If you’re unsure, get professional advice.
    *   **Metadata-Rich:** Accurate ISRC codes, BPM, key, genre, and detailed mood/instrumentation tags.
    *   **Stems Prepared:** Have high-quality WAV stems for each individual instrument or group of instruments.
2.  **Understand One-Stop Licensing:** Be prepared to offer both master and publishing rights clearance from a single point. This is non-negotiable for most supervisors.
3.  **Seek Out Infrastructure Platforms:** Look for services like SyncMaster that are specifically designed to bridge the gap between African composers and the global sync market. They handle the vetting, the pitching, and the administrative complexities.
4.  **Treat It Like a Business:** Respond to opportunities promptly. Understand the value of your music. Be professional in all your communications.

The era of African music being a niche or an afterthought in global productions is over. The talent is undeniable. Now, the infrastructure is catching up. By understanding the economics of sync and preparing your music to meet industry standards, you can shift your career from one of precarious gigging to one of sustainable, impactful financial success.
