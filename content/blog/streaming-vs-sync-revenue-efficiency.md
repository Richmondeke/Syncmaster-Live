## What Other Composers Can Learn

The success of these SyncMaster composers offers a clear roadmap for any African composer looking to tap into the global sync market:

1.  **Prioritize Rights Management:** Ensure you own 100% of your masters and compositions. If you have samples, clear them properly. Understand the difference between mechanical and performance royalties, and crucially, master and sync licenses. [INTERNAL LINK: Sync Licensing 101: The African Composer's Guide]
2.  **Master Your Metadata:** Treat metadata as a critical component of your release strategy. Accurate ISRC codes, detailed descriptive tags (mood, genre, instrumentation, energy level), and clear title/artist information are essential for discoverability.
3.  **Prepare Your Stems:** Organize your project files so you can easily export individual instrument tracks (stems) if requested. This shows you’re professional and ready for any production requirement.
4.  **Understand "One-Stop":** Supervisors prefer "one-stop" licenses because they simplify the clearance process. If you control both the master recording and the publishing, you are a one-stop shop.
5.  **Build a Catalogue, Not Just Singles:** Think about creating a body of work that can serve various production needs. Different moods, genres, and instrumental configurations will increase your chances of being matched.
