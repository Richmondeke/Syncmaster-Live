# Catapulting Talent: Analyzing a 1134% Increase in Artist Discovery

The global sync market is a powerful engine for artist discovery and revenue generation. While many independent African composers are focused on streaming royalties, the potential for significant income and audience growth through music licensing is often overlooked. A prime example of this phenomenon is the surge in artist discovery following a well-placed track. Let's break down a case study that highlights this potential and what it means for you.

## The Placement: What Happened

Consider the case of Baby Queen and her track "Colours of You." Placed in the critically acclaimed Netflix series *Heartstopper*, the song experienced an explosive increase in listener engagement. Within 28 days of the placement, the track saw over 1.5 million new listeners on Spotify, alongside a reported 1134% surge in Spotify discoveries.

This isn't an isolated incident. The Netflix series *Stranger Things* saw Kate Bush's 1985 track "Running Up That Hill" experience a 6,000% increase in first-time Spotify listeners, propelling it to #1 globally and its highest-ever chart position on the Billboard Hot 100. Similarly, Sia's "Breathe Me," after being placed in the *Six Feet Under* finale, sold over 1.2 million copies in the US and launched her international career. These examples demonstrate that a single, strategic placement can transform an artist's trajectory.
