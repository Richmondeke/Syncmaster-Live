# From Lagos to Showmax: Case Study of an Independent Composer Landing a Major Sync Deal

It’s a story we hear again and again in the global music industry: an independent artist, working from their home studio, lands a sync placement that changes their career trajectory. For African composers, this path is often obscured by a lack of infrastructure and understanding of the sync licensing world. But the opportunities are real, and the market is hungry for authentic sounds.

This is the story of how one composer, working with SyncMaster, secured a placement with Showmax, a leading African streaming service. It’s a testament to the power of prepared, professionally managed music meeting a direct market need.

## The Placement: What Happened

Our composer, based in South Africa, had been honing their craft for years, producing high-quality instrumental music with a distinct local flavour. They understood the creative side but found the business of licensing opaque.

A brief arrived through SyncMaster from a production company working on a new documentary series for Showmax. The request was specific: a 30-second piece of music that evoked a sense of hope and introspection, suitable for a pivotal scene in the series.

Within 24 hours of receiving the brief, SyncMaster had matched the composer’s available track. The production team at Showmax was presented with a curated selection, and our composer’s piece was chosen. This wasn't a cold pitch; it was a direct response to a specific creative need, delivered with all the necessary paperwork.

This placement, a 30-second instrumental cue, opened the door to significant financial reward and industry recognition.

## What Made It Possible

This placement didn't happen by accident. Several key factors aligned, driven by the SyncMaster infrastructure:

1.  **Rights-Cleared & Ready:** The composer had already gone through SyncMaster’s vetting process. This meant their music was confirmed as rights-clean, with masters and stems properly prepared. There were no uncleared samples or complex ownership issues that would have sunk the deal before it started. This addressed a primary pain point for supervisors: the fear of rights entanglements.

2.  **Professional Metadata:** Crucially, the composer’s track had comprehensive, cleaned metadata. This included ISRC codes, BPM, key, mood, and genre tags. When the brief came in, SyncMaster could instantly filter and match based on these precise details, ensuring the submitted track was a perfect fit. For supervisors, this means immediate discoverability.

3.  **One-Stop Clearance:** SyncMaster provides a one-stop licence. This means the composer controls all rights (publishing and master), simplifying the negotiation and clearance process immensely. Supervisors deal with one entity, one contract, and one payment. This is a massive time-saver and risk-reducer for busy production teams.

4.  **Speed and Responsiveness:** The entire process, from brief receipt to delivering the music, took less than 24 hours. The composer responded to the notification promptly, and SyncMaster facilitated the rapid delivery of stems and licensing terms. This speed is critical in the fast-paced world of film and TV production.

5.  **Direct Access to Briefs:** The composer didn't have to guess what supervisors wanted or send unsolicited demos. They received a direct brief tailored to their existing, ready-to-license catalogue. This structured approach replaces the inefficiency and low success rate of cold pitching.

## What It Paid

While exact figures for individual placements are often confidential, the industry benchmarks paint a clear picture of the financial potential.

A 30-second instrumental cue for a premium streaming service like Showmax can command a significant fee. Based on industry standards:

*   A single placement in a **Netflix scene** can range from **$5,000 to $20,000**.
*   A 30-second placement in a **premium TV ad** can range from **$10,000 to $75,000**.

Given Showmax's position in the African market and the nature of the placement within a documentary series, this composer likely earned a fee in the **upper thousands to low tens of thousands of US dollars** for a single 30-second track.

For context, the global sync licensing market generates over **$500 million annually**. This composer's single placement represents a substantial portion of what many independent musicians earn from streaming over an entire year. For example, earning $5,000 from sync could require over 1,000,000 streams on platforms like Spotify, depending on payout rates and deductions.

This single Showmax placement provided immediate, tangible financial benefit and validation for the composer’s work.

1.  **Your Music Needs to Be Sync-Ready:** This means having clean masters, full stem packages (drums, bass, melody, vocals, etc.), and, critically, a complete understanding and documentation of your rights. 3.  **Understand Rights and Be One-Stop:** The complexity of rights is the biggest hurdle in sync. If you can offer a one-stop licence, you are exponentially more attractive to supervisors. 4.  **Infrastructure Matters:** You can have world-class music, but without the infrastructure to present it professionally and legally, it won't get placed. This includes clear contracts, reliable file delivery, and a system for receiving and responding to briefs efficiently.

5.  **The Market is Global and African-Focused:** Productions are increasingly seeking authentic sounds from Africa. Services like Showmax are actively looking for local talent. ## How to Put Yourself in the Same Position

The path taken by this composer is replicable. It requires a shift from a purely creative mindset to a business-ready one.

1.  **Audit Your Catalogue:** Go through your existing music. Do you have stems? Is the metadata complete and accurate? Are there any potential rights issues? If you find gaps, address them now.
2.  **Get Your Rights in Order:** If you’ve collaborated or used samples, secure written agreements. Understand your publishing splits. The goal is to be able to confidently state you own 100% of the master and publishing for licensing.
3.  **Standardise Your Delivery:** Ensure your music is delivered in high-quality WAV format, with separate stems available upon request.
4.  **Seek Professional Infrastructure:** Platforms like SyncMaster are designed to bridge the gap between talented composers and the global sync market. They handle the complexities of rights clearance, metadata management, and direct submission to supervisors based on specific briefs.

The global sync market is worth hundreds of millions of dollars annually, and African music is a rapidly growing, in-demand asset. Composers who prepare their music and their business operations can tap into this lucrative market. This Showmax placement is just one example of what’s possible when talent meets the right infrastructure.
