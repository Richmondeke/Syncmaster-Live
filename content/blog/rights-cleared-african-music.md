## The Biggest Friction Points in Sourcing African Music

Despite the growing demand, sourcing rights-cleared African music presents unique challenges for music supervisors:

1.  **Rights Complexity:** African music often involves multiple rights holders, complex ownership structures, and a lack of standardised documentation. This makes achieving one-stop clearance incredibly difficult and time-consuming. Chasing down individual rights holders can take weeks, if not months.
2.  **Lack of Professional Infrastructure:** Many talented African composers operate without the established infrastructure common in Western markets. This means missing professional metadata, unorganized rights agreements, and a general lack of understanding about the sync licensing process itself.
3.  **"Wild West" Submissions:** Supervisors often receive submissions that are unvetted, uncleared, or lack basic information like stems or performer rights. This creates a significant administrative burden, forcing supervisors to spend valuable time on clearance instead of creative selection.
4.  **Finding Authenticity at Scale:** While individual artists like Tems, Burna Boy, and Wizkid have achieved global recognition, finding emerging, authentic voices at scale that are also sync-ready remains a significant challenge. You risk using music that feels derivative or is difficult to clear.

These friction points mean that even when a supervisor finds a track they love, the clearance process can be so arduous that they move on to an easier option.
