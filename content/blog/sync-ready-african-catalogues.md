# Curating High-Value Assets: The Transition to Sync-Ready Portfolios

The global music sync market is a multi-billion dollar industry, generating over $500M annually. For music supervisors, finding the right track that fits a brief, is legally clear, and delivers on time is paramount. Increasingly, the most exciting and authentic sounds are emerging from Africa. But accessing them requires more than just a great melody; it demands a sync-ready portfolio.

This isn't about finding obscure gems by chance. It's about understanding the infrastructure that transforms a composer's talent into a commercially viable asset. SyncMaster is built to bridge this gap, connecting global supervisors with African composers whose work is professionally prepared for licensing.

## The Artist and Their Background

Consider the journey of an independent composer in Lagos, Nigeria. For years, they've honed their craft, producing high-quality scores for local films, commercials, and independent projects. Their music is rich, diverse, and speaks to a global audience, yet they've never landed a significant international sync placement.

Their pain points are familiar: a lack of access to genuine briefs, confusion around rights and licensing complexities, and the sheer administrative burden of preparing music for a market they don't fully understand. They may have incredible tracks, but without proper metadata, clean stems, and a clear understanding of master vs. publishing rights, these tracks remain locked away from global opportunities. This composer possesses raw talent, but their portfolio isn't yet optimized for the demands of international sync.

## What it Means for African Music Globally

These placements signify a seismic shift. African music is no longer a niche genre waiting to be discovered; it is a powerful, commercially viable asset that global productions actively seek. Supervisors are looking for authentic sounds that resonate with diverse audiences, and African composers are delivering.

The traditional sync landscape often involves lengthy clearance processes, complex negotiations, and a lack of transparency. For a single 30-second placement in a premium TV ad, a supervisor might expect to pay between $10,000–$75,000. A placement in a Netflix scene can range from $5,000–$20,000. These figures highlight the immense financial potential that has historically been out of reach for many independent African composers due to infrastructure gaps.

By providing a platform that ensures music is "one-stop cleared"—meaning all rights are managed through a single point of contact—SyncMaster removes significant barriers for supervisors. This makes African music not just an attractive creative choice, but a practical and legally sound one.
