## What Made It Possible

Several critical factors converged to make this placement a reality, and they are the pillars upon which SyncMaster is built:

*   **Rights Clearance:** This is the absolute non-negotiable. For a music supervisor to even consider a track, they need absolute certainty that the rights are clean and that they can get a one-stop license. This means the composer owns 100% of the master and publishing rights, or has explicit permission from all co-owners. Many talented composers are disqualified immediately because their music is tied up with labels, uncleared samples exist, or multiple parties hold rights without clear agreements. SyncMaster vets every composer rigorously on this front. Our process ensures that when a brief arrives, the music submitted is legally clean. For the Lagos composer, their work was already rights-confirmed, meaning they retained 100% of their rights and masters. This dramatically streamlined the pitching process.

*   **Metadata Perfection:** In the world of sync, your metadata is your CV. It's how supervisors discover your music. This includes ISRC codes, BPM, key, genre, mood tags, instrumentation, and descriptive keywords. Without this detailed information, your track is effectively invisible. Supervisors don't have time to guess what your music is about; they need to match it to very specific project needs. SyncMaster works with composers to ensure their metadata is not just present, but optimized for discoverability. This means your music can be found when a supervisor is searching for something specific, like "hopeful organic afro-fusion" or "cinematic tense percussion."

*   **Stem Preparation:** For many sync placements, especially in film and TV, music editors need individual stems (separate audio files for drums, bass, vocals, synths, etc.). This allows them to mix the music precisely within the scene, adjust levels, or even isolate elements. While not always required for every brief, having stems ready significantly expands the types of opportunities you can pursue. SyncMaster guides composers on how to prepare and deliver these stems as part of our vetting process.

*   **Professional Submission & Speed:** Sync supervisors operate under intense deadlines. They need solutions, not more work. Submitting music that is unrequested, poorly formatted, or lacks essential information is a fast track to the 'no' pile. SyncMaster replaces the chaotic culture of cold-pitching with a structured, brief-matched system. When a supervisor submits a brief, we match it to pre-vetted composers and deliver curated options within 48 hours. The fastest brief-to-delivery for our network has been an astonishing 11 hours. This speed and professionalism are invaluable to supervisors.
