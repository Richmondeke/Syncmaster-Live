# Building a Bridge to Lagos: Our Month 1 Results and Lessons Learned

The global sync licensing market is a colossal engine, churning out over $500M annually. It’s the unseen force behind the music that defines our favourite films, TV shows, and advertisements. Yet, for many incredibly talented African composers, this lucrative market remains out of reach, a distant land accessible only through opaque channels or established gatekeepers. At SyncMaster, we’re building the infrastructure to change that.

This is our retrospective on Month 1. We’re documenting our journey—the applications, the vetting, the first placements, and the critical lessons learned. We believe in building in public, offering transparency to both the composers we serve and the supervisors we partner with.

## Why This Matters

The disparity in earning potential between streaming royalties and sync licensing is stark. A single 30-second placement in a premium TV ad can command between $10,000 and $75,000. A scene in a Netflix production might yield $5,000 to $20,000. Compare this to the fractions of a cent earned per stream, and the opportunity becomes clear. African music is globally resonant, as evidenced by placements like the one from a Lagos-based composer for a global NGO campaign, and a South African composer securing a placement on a Showmax production. The demand is undeniable. The gap is infrastructure.

## The Core Concept Explained

SyncMaster is not a music library. We are not a record label. We are the infrastructure that enables independent African composers to access and succeed in the global sync market. Our model is built on a few core principles:

1.  **Vetted Composers:** We only work with composers whose music is production-ready. This means professional quality, clear rights, and readiness to provide stems and clean metadata.
2.  **Brief-Matched Submissions:** We don’t do cold submissions. Supervisors send us briefs, and we match pre-vetted composers to those specific needs. This is how we achieve our rapid turnaround times—as fast as 11 hours from brief to delivery.
3.  **One-Stop Clearance:** We ensure every track pitched is one-stop cleared. This means the composer controls both the master recording and the publishing rights, simplifying negotiations for supervisors and eliminating the administrative headache of split ownership. This is crucial for high-stakes productions where clearance delays can cost millions. [INTERNAL LINK: What is a one-stop licence?]
4.  **Transparency:** We document our process. We share our traction, our challenges, and our successes. We believe this open approach builds trust and demystifies the sync process.

## What African Composers Need to Know

For independent African composers, understanding the sync landscape is the first step to unlocking significant income streams. The key elements supervisors look for are:

*   **Rights Clarity:** This is non-negotiable. Supervisors need to know, with absolute certainty, that you own 100% of the master and publishing rights. Uncleared samples or label-held masters are immediate disqualifiers.
*   **Metadata:** Your music needs to be discoverable. This includes accurate tagging for genre, mood, instrumentation, tempo (BPM), and key. Crucially, every track must have a unique ISRC code. [INTERNAL LINK: ISRC codes explained]
*   **File Preparation:** Supervisors need high-quality audio files. This means providing lossless formats (like WAV) and, ideally, separate stems for individual instruments or groups of instruments. This allows for greater flexibility in editing during post-production. [INTERNAL LINK: How to prepare your stems for sync]
*   **Professionalism:** This extends to communication, turnaround time on requests, and understanding the brief.

Many talented composers possess exceptional musical skills but lack awareness of these technical and administrative requirements. This is the infrastructure gap SyncMaster addresses.

## Practical Steps

If you are an African composer looking to break into sync, consider these immediate actions:

1.  **Audit Your Catalogue:** For every track you have, ask yourself: Do I own 100% of this? Are there any samples? Is the metadata complete and accurate? Do I have high-quality WAV files and stems?
2.  **Register Your Works:** Ensure all your compositions are registered with the relevant copyright societies.
3.  **Understand Rights:** Educate yourself on master vs. publishing rights and the concept of "one-stop" licensing.
4.  **Network:** Connect with other composers, music supervisors (where possible), and industry professionals online.

This self-assessment is vital. It highlights where the friction points are before you even engage with a platform like SyncMaster.
