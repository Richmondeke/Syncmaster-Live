This guide breaks down exactly what you need to know to ensure you collect every cent of your sync royalties.

## The Core Concept Explained

Your PRO is your official representative for collecting performance royalties. When your music is played publicly – on radio, TV, in live venues, or even online streaming services in certain contexts – your PRO collects the fees and distributes them to you. This includes royalties generated from sync licenses.

Think of your PRO as your rights' accountant. They track where your music is used in public broadcasting and performance settings and ensure you get paid. They have agreements with other PROs worldwide, creating a global network for royalty collection. For example, if your music is licensed in the US for a TV show, the US PRO (like ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC) will collect the performance royalties and then pass them on to your PRO, who then pays you.

This is crucial for sync because a sync license covers the *synchronization* of your music with visual media. However, there's often a separate *performance royalty* generated when that visual media is broadcast or streamed. Your PRO is the key to unlocking these performance royalties.

## What African Composers Need to Know

As an independent African composer, you likely deal with multiple PROs across the continent, such as COSON in Nigeria, SAMRO in South Africa, or MCSK in Kenya. The first step is to ensure you are registered with the PRO that covers your primary territory.

**Key points for African composers:**

*   **Understand the Distinction:** A sync license fee is paid by the music supervisor or production company directly to the rights holder (you, or your publisher). This is for the *right to use* your music. Performance royalties are generated when the work is *broadcast or publicly performed*, and these are collected by your PRO. ## Practical Steps to Get Registered

Getting registered with your PRO is a straightforward process, but it requires attention to detail.

1.  **Identify Your Primary PRO:** Determine which PRO is the primary collecting society for your country of residence.
    *   **Nigeria:** Copyright Society of Nigeria (COSON)
    *   **South Africa:** Southern African Music Rights Organisation (SAMRO)
    *   **Kenya:** Music Copyright Society of Kenya (MCSK)
    *   *And others across the continent.*
2.  **Visit Their Website:** Navigate to your PRO's official website. Look for sections like "Register as a Member," "Composer Registration," or "Join Us."
3.  **Complete the Application:** You'll typically need to provide:
    *   Your personal details (name, address, contact information).
    *   Banking details for royalty payments.
    *   Information about your musical works: titles, writers, publishers (if any), and crucially, your ISRC codes for each track.
4.  **Submit Supporting Documents:** Some PROs may require proof of identity or documentation of your published works.
5.  **Understand Your Agreement:** Read the membership agreement carefully. It will outline your rights, responsibilities, and the PRO's fee structure for collecting royalties.
6.  **Register Your Catalogue:** Once you are a member, actively register all your existing and new compositions with the PRO. This is the critical step that allows them to track and collect royalties for you.

**Important Note:** Some African composers may have previously registered with organizations that focus solely on mechanical or broadcast rights. Ensure you are registered with the PRO specifically responsible for public performance royalties. This is a common point of confusion.
