## The Core Concept Explained: Sync Licensing vs. Streaming Royalties

Understanding the fundamental difference between streaming royalties and sync licensing fees is key.

**Streaming Royalties:** These are payments made to artists and rights holders based on the number of times a track is played on a streaming platform. The rates are typically very low, negotiated between the platforms and labels/distributors. While cumulative plays can generate substantial income, each individual play contributes a tiny fraction. The Spotify Loud & Clear Nigeria 2025 report highlights the scale of these plays for Nigerian artists, but the revenue per play remains the limiting factor.

**Sync Licensing Fees:** These are fees paid for the *permission* to use a piece of music in a visual medium. The fee is negotiated based on various factors, including the prominence of the music, the type of media (film, TV, ad, game), the territory of use, and the duration of the license. Crucially, sync deals often involve a "one-stop" clearance, meaning you can license both the master recording and the publishing rights from a single point of contact. This simplifies the process for supervisors and often commands higher fees.

The Spotify Loud & Clear Nigeria 2025 data shows millions of daily streams, but these streams don't automatically translate into sync placements. The infrastructure for discovering, vetting, and pitching music for sync is entirely different from the infrastructure that powers streaming distribution.

*   **Vetting for Sync Readiness:** Not all music is "sync-ready." Supervisors need tracks that are professionally produced, mixed, and mastered, with clear metadata, and often, separate stem files.
