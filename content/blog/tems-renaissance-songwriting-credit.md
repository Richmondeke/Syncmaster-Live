# Songwriting as Asset Development: Tems' Contributions to Renaissance

The global sync market is a multi-billion dollar industry where music finds its place in film, television, ads, and games. For African composers, understanding this market means seeing their work not just as art, but as a valuable asset. Tems' contributions to Beyoncé's *Renaissance* album offer a powerful case study in how an artist's songwriting prowess can translate into significant global opportunities. This isn't about luck; it's about the inherent value of well-crafted music and the infrastructure that allows it to be recognized and licensed.

## The Artist and Their Background

Tems, born Temilade Openiyi, has emerged as a singular voice in global music. Hailing from Lagos, Nigeria, her distinct vocal style, introspective lyrics, and genre-bending sound have captivated audiences worldwide. Before her global breakthrough, Tems was already a respected artist in Nigeria, known for hits like "Try Me" and "Mr. Rebel." Her journey is a testament to the growing power of African artists on the international stage, demonstrating a unique ability to blend contemporary Afrobeats influences with soul, R&B, and pop sensibilities.

Her distinctive approach to songwriting—often characterized by raw emotion, relatable narratives, and a confident yet vulnerable delivery—has resonated deeply with listeners and industry tastemakers alike. This authentic voice is precisely what makes her music so attractive to supervisors seeking music that can add depth and character to visual media.

## The Placement or Moment

Tems' involvement in Beyoncé's critically acclaimed 2022 album *Renaissance* marked a significant moment in her career and for African representation in global music production. She co-wrote and featured on the track "MOVE," alongside the legendary Grace Jones. This collaboration placed Tems directly within one of the most anticipated and culturally significant musical projects of the year.

While *Renaissance* itself is a studio album, the impact of its tracks ripples outwards. Beyoncé's music, especially projects with such immense cultural weight, frequently finds its way into various forms of media. The sonic textures, lyrical themes, and collaborative spirit of *Renaissance* create a rich tapestry of potential sync opportunities. Tracks from the album have been considered for use in trailers, promotional materials, and potentially future film or television projects associated with the *Renaissance* world tour or Beyoncé's broader artistic output. Each of these potential placements represents a tangible licensing opportunity, where Tems' songwriting contribution would be valued and compensated.

For instance, a single 30-second placement in a premium TV ad can generate between $10,000–$75,000. Similarly, a scene placement in a major Netflix production can yield $5,000–$20,000. While specific sync deals for "MOVE" or other *Renaissance* tracks may not be publicly detailed, Tems' involvement in such a high-profile project inherently increases the perceived value and marketability of her songwriting catalogue.

## What it Means for African Music Globally

Tems' role on *Renaissance* is more than just a personal achievement; it's a powerful signal about the evolving landscape of global music. It demonstrates that African artists are not just consumers or influencers of global trends, but active creators and collaborators at the highest echelons of the industry. Her presence alongside Beyoncé and Grace Jones on a track signifies a recognition of her unique talent and artistic vision as being on par with global superstars.

This elevates the perception of African music as a whole. It moves the narrative from "niche genre" to "essential creative force." For music supervisors and industry professionals, it reinforces that authentic, high-quality music from Africa is not only available but is actively shaping major global cultural moments. The global sync market, which generates over $500M annually, is constantly seeking fresh, impactful sounds. Tems' success makes it easier for supervisors to justify exploring African music catalogues, knowing that artists possess the talent and credibility to contribute to major productions.

This wider acceptance opens doors for other African composers. When artists like Tems, Fireboy DML (who performed at the UEFA Champions League Final), or Burna Boy (with numerous film and brand placements) gain global traction, it validates the entire continent's musical output. It reduces the perceived risk for supervisors and publishers when considering African talent.
