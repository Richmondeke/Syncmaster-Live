## What Made It Possible

What separates these placements from the thousands of tracks languishing in streaming obscurity? It’s not just about talent; it’s about readiness and access.

### 1. Rights Clarity: The Non-Negotiable Foundation

The biggest hurdle for many independent African composers is navigating music rights. When a music supervisor or production company looks for music, their absolute top priority is clear, clean rights. They cannot afford delays or legal disputes.

This means:
*   **100% Ownership:** The composer must own all rights to their master recording and composition.
*   **No Uncleared Samples:** Any sample used must have explicit clearance.
*   **One-Stop Shop:** Ideally, the deal can be cleared with a single point of contact, avoiding complex negotiations with multiple publishers or rights holders.

This is why SyncMaster vets every composer rigorously. We ensure that every track we pitch is rights-confirmed. Our composers retain 100% of their rights. For the Lagos composer and the South African composer, their music was already rights-cleared and owned outright, removing the biggest barrier. [INTERNAL LINK: What is a one-stop licence?]

### 2. Metadata and File Preparation: Making Discovery Easy

Supervisors work under tight deadlines. They need music that is easy to find, easy to understand, and easy to use. This requires meticulous metadata and proper file preparation.

*   **ISRC Codes:** Unique identifiers for master recordings are essential for tracking and royalty collection.
*   **Detailed Tagging:** Composers need to tag their music with accurate genre, mood, instrumentation, and lyrical themes.
*   **Stems:** For film and TV, supervisors often need individual instrument tracks (stems) for editing and mixing.

At SyncMaster, we guide composers through this process. We don’t just accept MP3s. We ensure that tracks have proper ISRC codes, detailed descriptive tags, and that stems are ready. ## What It Paid: The Real Value

Let's break down the financial reality, comparing streaming earnings to sync fees.

**Streaming Reality:**
*   A typical payout from major streaming platforms is around $0.003 to $0.005 per stream.
*   After deductions from labels, distributors, and publishers, the composer might see only 10-20% of that.
*   To earn $5,000 at $0.004 per stream, a track needs approximately **1.25 million streams**.
*   To earn $10,000, you're looking at **2.5 million streams**.
