# What Happens During SyncMaster Onboarding? Inside Our 7-Day Vetting

You've poured your heart and soul into your music. You know it has the quality and emotion to connect with a global audience. But how do you get it from your studio into the world's biggest films, TV shows, and ad campaigns? The answer lies in understanding the sync licensing infrastructure, and specifically, what makes your music ready for it. This is where the SyncMaster onboarding process comes in.

## Why This Matters

The global sync market generates over $500 million annually. For context, a single 30-second placement in a premium TV ad can earn between $10,000–$75,000. A scene in a major production like Netflix can range from $5,000–$20,000. These are figures far beyond typical streaming royalties. However, accessing this market requires more than just great music; it demands professionalism, clear rights, and precise metadata. SyncMaster exists to bridge that gap for African composers. Our onboarding is not a barrier; it's the gateway to making your music commercially viable on a global scale.

## The Core Concept Explained

SyncMaster is an infrastructure platform, not a music library or a label. We connect African composers directly to global music supervisors. Our unique value is in preparing your music to meet the exact demands of the sync market. This means ensuring your tracks are:

*   **Rights-Clear:** Every composer on our platform is confirmed to own 100% of their master and publishing rights. This is crucial for "one-stop" clearance, which music supervisors desperately need.
*   **Metadata-Clean:** Your music needs to be discoverable. This involves correct ISRC codes, accurate genre tagging, mood descriptors, and tempo information.
*   **Stems-Ready:** For many placements, especially in film and TV, individual instrument stems are required. We ensure you have these prepared.
*   **Brief-Matched:** We don't rely on cold submissions. When a music supervisor has a brief, we match pre-vetted composers and their music to it, allowing for a rapid, targeted response.

Our onboarding process is designed to ensure every composer we work with meets these essential criteria. It’s a 7-day vetting period that transforms your music from a passion project into a professional sync asset.

## Practical Steps

Our 7-day vetting process is structured to be thorough yet efficient. Here's what you can expect:

1.  **Application Review:** You submit your music and basic composer information. Our team reviews your application to ensure your genre and quality align with the global sync market we serve.
2.  **Rights Verification:** This is paramount. We work with you to confirm you hold 100% of your master and publishing rights. This might involve reviewing existing registration details or guiding you through the process if needed. We need to ensure there are no uncleared samples or co-ownership issues that would prevent a one-stop license.
3.  **Metadata Audit:** We meticulously check your track metadata. This includes ensuring you have ISRC codes assigned, accurate genre and mood tags, and correct artist/composer information. If there are gaps, we'll provide clear guidance on how to fill them.
4.  **Stem Preparation Check:** We verify that you have high-quality individual stems for your tracks. If not, we'll guide you on how to export them correctly. This is essential for supervisors who may need to edit or re-mix parts of your music.
5.  **Quality & Sync-Readiness Assessment:** Our team listens critically to your music, assessing its production quality, sonic clarity, and suitability for sync briefs. We're looking for tracks that are impactful, well-mixed, and have a professional sheen.
6.  **Final Confirmation:** Once all checks are complete, your tracks are officially vetted and ready to be matched with incoming briefs.

This entire process is designed to take approximately 7 days from the moment you submit your application.
